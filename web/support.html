Getting started

1. Scan a piece of fruit or a leaf

Open the app, point the camera at the fruit or foliage, and hold steady in even light. OrchardEye reads color, greenness, and lesions on-device and returns a plain-language verdict — no internet required.

2. (Optional) Pair the spectral sensor

The OrchardEye NIR sensor adds ripeness and Brix readings. Power it on, then allow Bluetooth when the app asks. Once paired, a scan fuses the camera and sensor into a single verdict.

3. Review trends

Saved scans build a history so you can watch ripeness and disease pressure change across the season — all stored on your device.

Frequently asked questions

Does OrchardEye work without internet?

Yes. Scanning, analysis, and voice guidance all run offline on your phone. The only feature that uses the network is optional local weather, and the app falls back to cached and default data when you're off-grid.

The sensor won't connect over Bluetooth.

Make sure the sensor is powered and within a few feet, that Bluetooth is enabled in iOS Settings, and that you granted OrchardEye Bluetooth permission (Settings → OrchardEye). Toggling the sensor off and on, then re-opening the app, resolves most pairing hiccups.

Why does the app ask for my location?

Only to fetch local weather for better spray-timing and disease-pressure guidance. It's optional — decline it and everything else keeps working. See our Privacy Policy for details.

My scan verdict seems off.

Lighting matters. Scan in even, natural light, fill the frame with the fruit or leaf, and avoid deep shadows or glare. If you're using the sensor, keep it flush against the fruit inside its dark shroud so ambient light doesn't skew the reading.

Where are my scans stored? Can I delete them?

On your device. Delete an individual scan from the in-app history, or remove the app to erase all of its data. We keep no server-side copy.

Is my data private?

Yes — scans are analyzed on-device, there are no accounts, and we don't sell data or run ad/analytics trackers. Full details are in our Privacy Policy.

What hardware do I need?

Just an iPhone for camera-based scanning. For ripeness and Brix, the optional OrchardEye sensor kit (AS7265x spectral sensor, ESP32 BLE bridge, refractometer, 3D-printed shroud) runs roughly $118 in parts.
